# Layered Healthcare Access Comparison  
### Portugal and the Netherlands  

This repository documents the research collaboration between **Baoshan Liang** and **Jack Martin** on **layered healthcare access systems**, with a comparative focus on **Portugal** and **the Netherlands**.

---

## Overview  

Healthcare access is not a single dimension but a **multi-layered system** shaped by policy, geography, socioeconomic status, and institutional design.  

This project develops a **layered analytical framework** to compare how access to healthcare is structured and experienced across two European systems:

- Portugal  
- The Netherlands  

The goal is to identify **structural similarities, bottlenecks, and policy-relevant differences**.

---

## Research Motivation  

European healthcare systems are often described as universal, yet **access inequalities persist across multiple layers**, including:

- Legal entitlement  
- Physical accessibility  
- Financial barriers  
- Administrative complexity  
- Cultural and informational barriers  

For example, Portugal provides broadly universal coverage through its National Health Service, but **practical barriers such as administrative and social inequalities still affect access** :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.  

This project builds on such insights by explicitly modeling access as a **layered system rather than a binary condition**.

---

## Conceptual Framework  

We define healthcare access as a composition of interacting layers:

### 1. Entitlement Layer  
- Legal rights to healthcare  
- Insurance coverage or registration requirements  

### 2. Availability Layer  
- Distribution of hospitals, clinics, and providers  
- Capacity constraints  

### 3. Accessibility Layer  
- Travel time and geographic reach  
- Urban vs rural disparities  

### 4. Affordability Layer  
- Out-of-pocket costs  
- Co-payments and insurance structures  

### 5. Acceptability Layer  
- Cultural compatibility  
- Language barriers  
- Trust in institutions  

---

## Comparative Focus  

### Portugal  
- National Health Service (SNS) with universalist principles  
- Strong formal entitlement  
- Persistent barriers for vulnerable populations  

### The Netherlands  
- Mandatory insurance-based system  
- Strong primary care gatekeeping  
- Highly structured provider networks  

The comparison highlights how **different institutional designs produce different access bottlenecks across layers**.
